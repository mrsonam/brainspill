# Context

## Product Summary

The product is a desktop-first collaborative infinite canvas for creative moodboards and project whiteboarding. It starts as a calm blank workspace and expands naturally as users zoom, pan, paste, upload, and arrange content.

The app should combine the expressive freedom of a visual moodboard with the structure and collaboration patterns of a modern whiteboard. Users should be able to capture ideas quickly as sticky notes, images, transparent stickers, shapes, arrows, and lightweight drawings without feeling forced into a document, slide, or rigid board layout.

## Target Users

- Creative individuals collecting references, inspiration, screenshots, stickers, and ideas.
- Small teams planning projects visually with notes, arrows, images, and shared context.
- Designers, founders, students, researchers, and makers who want a fast place to think spatially.
- Users who like Figma-style navigation but want a lighter, more personal idea canvas.

## Primary Jobs To Be Done

- Capture ideas immediately without setup.
- Arrange thoughts and visual references spatially.
- Collaborate live with others on a shared visual board.
- Zoom out to see the bigger picture and zoom in to refine details.
- Switch between freeform blank canvas and grid-assisted layout.

## Product Positioning

The app should feel closer to a creative workspace than a corporate meeting board. It should be polished, responsive, and low-friction, with collaboration strong enough for teams but a visual tone that still suits personal thought boards and moodboards.

## Assumptions

- Desktop web is the launch priority.
- Users will sign in and save boards to a cloud-backed account.
- Realtime collaboration is a long-term core differentiator, not an add-on.
- The first release should prioritize quality of canvas interaction over breadth of tools.
- Uploaded and pasted images are central to the experience.

## Non-Goals For The First Release

- Mobile-first editing.
- Complex diagramming parity with Miro or Lucidchart.
- Full design-tool parity with Figma.
- Marketplace, plugin system, or public template gallery.
- AI features such as OCR, clustering, image generation, or background removal.
- Heavy enterprise administration, audit logs, or organization billing.
